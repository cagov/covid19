Many California schools, colleges, and universities are closing or moving online during the COVID-19 outbreak. The California Department of Public Health has released Guidance for Schools and Guidance for Colleges and Universities.

The governor’s executive order ensures public schools will retain full funding, even if they close campuses. For current information about closures and their impact on the school year, check your local school district.

For colleges and universities, see:

  • University of California
  • California State University (CSU)
  • California Community Colleges

Questions and answers

Will families impacted by closed schools still have access to free or reduced lunches?

The governor’s executive order ensures public schools will retain full funding. Check your local school district for more information on how and where to receive services, including school meals.

How will students with disabilities receive services during school closures?

If a school keeps teaching during a physical closure (by moving classes online, for example), the school must ensure students with disabilities have equal access to the same opportunities. More information is available via the Department of Education.

Will there be ELPAC testing? When?

The ELPAC (English Language Proficiency Assessments for California) administration will arrange testing after a school closure related to COVID-19. If you are a school district please call 1-800-955-2954 or email caltac@ets.org for assistance.

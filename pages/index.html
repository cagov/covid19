Do:

  • Stay home if you can
  • Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Cover your cough or sneeze with an elbow or tissue
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces
  • If soap and water aren’t available, use alcohol-based hand sanitizer

Don’t:

  • Shake hands
  • Touch your face
  • Go near people who are sick
  • Go to the doctor if you aren’t sick
  • Stockpile masks or gloves

What you can do

Stay home Avoid group events Testing and treatment Apply for unemployment insurance Apply for disability insurance benefits

Services and information

Education

Many schools are closing campuses temporarily. Check your district for the latest.

Employment and taxes

Employees should stay home if sick. State tax deadlines have been extended to June 15.

Health care

California is working to ensure everyone has healthcare coverage and access to testing.

Public places

Stay updated on recommendations to reduce further spread of coronavirus.

Get local information

Find and follow local public health authorities for the most updated guidance.

Latest numbers

As of March 14, there are 335 positive cases and 6 deaths in California. See the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) for the latest data.

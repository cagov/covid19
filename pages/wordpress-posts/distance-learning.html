We’ve identified resources for teachers who want to strengthen their remote teaching skills.

The Governor’s Task Force on Business & Jobs Recovery identified support for educators as a critical factor for California’s recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic. For schools that have not reopened for in-person instruction, educators continue to navigate the challenges of providing high quality distance learning. There are many distance learning resources available and the following list was selected based on quality and scope.

San José State University (SJSU) Online Teacher Academy

The SJSU Lurie College of Education K-12 Online Teaching Academy was established in Summer 2020 in response to the inequities in learning exacerbated by the COVID-19 crisis and was intended to create free opportunities for pre-service teacher candidates and current teachers to strengthen their online skills. The 23 Summer 2020 webinar recordings feature a wide range of teachers, professors, and other practitioners who discuss topics like:

  • How to build equity
  • Employing emancipatory pedagogies
  • Utilizing a multitude of online platforms

Lurie College is organizing additional webinars for January and February 2021.

View webinar series

UC San Diego Teaching Online Essentials

UC San Diego Extension has offered a certificate program in online teaching for more than 15 years. When the pandemic struck, Extension’s Education and Community Outreach (ECO) Department distilled this proven curriculum into an intensive training experience called Teaching Online Essentials.

Teachers and parents can find best practices in online and hybrid education, emphasizing creativity, connectivity and transformative experiences geared to the needs of diverse learners.

CTA, Stanford SCOPE, and NBRC Instructional Leadership Corps

The California Teachers Association (CTA), Stanford Center for Opportunity Policy in Education (SCOPE) and the National Board Resource Center (NBRC) educator-led Instructional Leadership Corps (ILC) grows the ability of local educators to enrich instruction and assessment practices in their schools by creating professional learning experiences and resources for other educators.

They offer a variety of resources, including the ILC Professional Learning Handbook, Teacher Tools for Practice, and member-led distance learning webinars.

Access available resources

CTA Instruction and Professional Development Team

The California Teachers Association (CTA) Instruction and Professional Development Team connects 310,000 CTA members with technical assistance, professional learning, resources, and networks to help them grow and thrive as professional educators in strong public schools.

Access professional learning sessions led by current practitioners focused on effective practices for engagement, instruction, and assessment for diverse learners in the context of distance and hybrid learning.

Access professional learning

Google for Education resources

Google Teacher Center

The Google Teacher Center provides free training and resources designed to help educators of all levels use technology to make teaching and learning more effective, efficient, and engaging—however your classroom looks this year.

  • Find quick tips and resources for tools like Classroom, Slides, and Meet with our product guides.
  • Discover new strategies for keeping students engaged while teaching remotely in our Distance Learning and Inspiring Creativity training courses.
  • Learn to integrate Google tools in your classroom in the Fundamentals and Advanced training courses.

No login is necessary to access content, but is recommended to track progress. Google Certified Educator exams require payment to obtain credentials and take the exam (recommended through Kryterion).

Apple Education resources

Apple has a wide range of programs and resources to help schools, teachers, and parents create engaging learning experiences at home.

The Apple Teacher Learning Center provides free online professional learning to help teachers use iPad and Mac to inspire creative teaching and learning, no matter where learning is happening. It offers unlimited access to learning materials and content, as well as lesson ideas.

Apple Education Learning Series videos are designed to help educators use the built-in features of their Apple products. A collection of guides for both teachers and students help infuse creativity and engagement into any lesson and support workflows to stay on task, stay organized, and create productive learning environments. Creative Activities for Kids and Quick Start to Code guides keep young learners constructive and creative with iPad.

Apple Professional Learning Specialists hold free weekly virtual conferences and one-on-one coaching sessions to support personal learning. Sign up for free Apple Professional Learning Virtual Conferences. To request a free one-on-one, 30-minute virtual coaching session to support personal learning, email AppleProfessionalLearning@apple.com.

Health professionals, California needs you!

California is responding to an increase in the number of people who urgently need health care due to the COVID-19 outbreak. To relieve the pressure on our health care system, we’re reallocating health care professionals throughout California to treat people affected by COVID-19, while also ensuring non COVID-19 cases get the proper care they need.

To ensure adequate staff for health care sites throughout California, we’re calling on healthcare professionals to register today.

We need:

  • Nurses (RN, LVN, CNA) 
  • Emergency medical technicians 
  • Nurse practitioners 
  • Paramedics
  • Physicians (MD, DO, psychiatrist) 
  • Respiratory therapists 
  • Behavioral health professionals

How to join California Health Corps

Step 1 – Confirm your eligibility

To be eligible for COVID-19 emergency medical staffing roles, you must: 

  • Be 18 years of age or over 
  • Be eligible to work in the United States 
  • Have a valid ID and social security number 
  • Have a valid California license for clinical practice (if you are a MD, DO, etc.)
  • Have no negative licensure/certification actions (for licensed/certified professionals) 

Step 2 – Register for Disaster Healthcare Volunteers (DHV) 

This is the system we’ll use to verify your medical license and identify your deployment preferences. 

Register

Step 3 – Once verified, we will ask you to submit an application

You will get instructions for how to apply through CalCareers.

Step 4 – Onboard 

To complete the hiring process, you will need to complete some standard employment documentation. Then, you will receive training on how to sign up for shifts, what to expect, how to request lodging and reimbursement, and more. 

Step 5 – Deploy 

  • When you are officially approved to work for CA Health Corps, you can sign up for shifts, view upcoming shifts, submit hours worked, view helpful resources, and more on your personal online portal.
  • We try to match you with facilities nearest to you, or we can arrange for you to be added to facilities statewide. 
  • CA Health Corps Deployment Team will support you in signing up for shifts, arranging for lodging, reimbursing your travel expenses, answering your specific questions, tracking your hours, and more.

Questions and answers

What is the California Health Corps? 

The state of California established California Health Corps in April 2020 to meet additional health care needs during the COVID-19 pandemic. 

How does the California Health Corps work? 

When facilities in California have exhausted local and regional resources, California Health Corps is called upon for additional support. As a Health Corps participant, you will be able to sign up for shifts based on your availability, willingness to travel, and skillset. 

What is my role in the California Health Corps? 

CA Health Corps participants are temporary state employees. As a CA Health Corps participant, your work will directly help those most impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. You will have the freedom to work when and where you choose. 

Can a doctor with out of state license or foreign medical graduate without California license work for the California Health Corps?

No. To be eligible for COVID-19 emergency medical staffing roles, you must: 

  • Be eligible to work in the United States  
  • Have a valid California License for clinical practice (if you are a MD, DO, etc.)

What are the pay and benefits for California Health Corps professionals?

Salary rates for most of the health care professionals being hired for this emergency are listed in the CalHR Pay Scales (pages 5.12 and 5.13). In addition, CalHR has established pay rates for medical roles that don’t have a corresponding regular classification and pay schedule, such as paramedics and EMTs. CalHR will determine specific salary and benefits after conducting a review of each application.

Some healthcare professions are not listed. Are health professionals who are not listed (e.g., Medical Technicians, Lab Technicians, etc.) eligible for the California Health Corps?

California Health Corps are currently only accepting applications for the healthcare professions listed on the website. However, we are constantly re-evaluating our needs during this fluid situation and will open the registration for other professionals, if the need arises.

When can I expect to hear a response after completing my California Health Corps registration?

Currently we anticipate the process may take up to two weeks, since an incredible number of responses are received and need to prioritize certain parts of the state, where the needs are the greatest. We will begin to contact the volunteers after verification of credentials, and based on current and anticipated needs.

How do I return to the California Health Corps application after logging out?

Visit https://healthcarevolunteers.ca.gov/ and use the account you created while applying to correct or update any information you want.

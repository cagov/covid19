Everyone who has symptoms and tests positive for COVID-19 should seek treatment. Medications to treat COVID-19 are free, safe, and effective. They can stop COVID-19 illness from getting serious, and can help prevent hospitalization and death. Medication is widely available and recommended for most people 12 years and older.

COVID-19 medications work best when taken within 5-7 days of when symptoms begin. So as soon as you feel sick, act quickly.

Test it

If you have COVID-19 symptoms, take a COVID-19 test as soon as possible. Act quickly because COVID-19 medications must be taken within 5-7 days of when symptoms begin. This does not have to be a lab test. At-home test results are okay. For more information on how to get tested, click here. 

If you test positive for COVID-19, seek treatment right away. Do not wait for symptoms to get worse. Stay home and isolate to prevent spreading the disease to others. Even if you are vaccinated or have had COVID-19 before, you should isolate for at least 5 full days. Learn about isolation guidelines.

If your test is negative but you continue to have symptoms, test again 24-48 hours later. Low amounts of virus early in infection can be missed, and you can test positive a few days later.

Treat it

  • Most COVID-19 treatments are pills that you take at home. COVID-19 treatments are free in California. You don’t need to have insurance or be a US citizen.
  • COVID-19 medications are effective at stopping COVID-19 illness from getting serious. They can lower the risk of serious illness, hospitalization, and death from COVID-19 by half or more.
  • Medications may help you test negative sooner, and may reduce the risk of long COVID symptoms.
    • They work best when started as soon as possible, within the first 5-7 days of symptoms. Don’t wait for symptoms to get worse.
    • You must have COVID-19 symptoms and should have a positive COVID-19 test. Use an at-home test or learn how to get tested.
  • COVID-19 treatment is recommended for most adults and anyone 12 years and older with certain health conditions. A doctor can tell you if you are eligible to receive COVID-19 medications and which type is best for you. If you test positive for COVID-19, have symptoms, and meet the following criteria, you should seek treatment:
    • You are 50 years old or older –OR–
    • You are unvaccinated or not up-to-date with COVID-19 vaccinations –OR–
    • You have a health conditions that increases risk. These include obesity, asthma, diabetes, high blood pressure, lack of physical activity, smoking, and mental health conditions such as depression. Learn more about medical conditions that increase the risk of serious illness from COVID-19. 
  • COVID-19 medications can help whether you are vaccinated or not. Both people who have been vaccinated or not vaccinated for COVID-19 can benefit from COVID-19 treatments.

How to find treatment

Contact your doctor or insurance provider for an appointment. Video or phone appointments are fine.

Call the statewide COVID-19 hotline at 833-422-4255.

Find a Test-to-Treat location near you. These sites can prescribe COVID-19 medications. To find a site use the COVID-19 Test to Treat Locator.

Some pharmacies may be able to give you a prescription for treatment. Call your pharmacy to see if they offer this service.

If you don’t have insurance or the options above don’t work:

  • Click here to make a free virtual COVID-19 appointment.
  • Call 833-686-5051 to make a phone or video appointment with California’s COVID-19 telehealth service

Note: If you are not able to get a prescription from your doctor, but think you should be eligible for treatment, click here or call 833-686-5051 to make a phone or video appointment with California’s free COVID-19 provider. You can also show your doctor CDPH’s recommendations on treatment.

Beat it

COVID-19 medications have been shown to stop COVID-19 illness from getting serious. They can lower the risk of serious illness, hospitalization, and death from COVID-19 by half or more. They may help you test negative sooner. Early evidence also suggests COVID-19 treatment may decrease the risk of developing long COVID symptoms. Long COVID symptoms can last for weeks, months, or years after a COVID-19 infection.

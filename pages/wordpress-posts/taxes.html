California is providing tax relief during the pandemic.

On this page:

  • Extended filing deadline
  • Individual taxes
  • Small business taxes
  • Questions and answers

Extended filing deadline

The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA) is providing a three-month extension for individuals and businesses to file and pay their taxes and fees originally due between December 15, 2020, and April 30, 2021. Taxpayers reporting less than $1,000,000 in taxes and fees on a return originally due during this period are not required to seek an extension from CDTFA. Relief will be automatically provided.

Individual taxes

The California Earned Income Tax Credit and Young Child Tax Credit are now available to taxpayers who have an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). These tax credits can be claimed on 2020 individual tax returns (which are filed in 2021).

You can also use your 2019 income to claim the federal Earned Income Tax Credit and Child Tax Credit on your 2020 taxes. This lets you claim these credits even if your income was lower in 2020 due to job loss or underemployment.

Small business taxes

California and the federal government are providing tax assistance and benefits to small businesses. This includes:

  • The Small Business Hiring Tax Credit, a $1,000 tax credit for each new employee hired by small businesses (up to $100,000 per business)
    • To apply for this credit, visit the CDTFA website
  • Interest-free deferral of sales/use tax up to $50,000 for businesses with less than $5 million in taxable sales
  • Excluding forgiven Paycheck Protection Program loans from state gross income
  • The federal Employee Retention Tax Credit, available through July 1, 2021, of up to $14,000
  • Federal tax credits to small businesses for COVID-19 employee paid leave

Questions and answers

Are there any tax deductions for people affected by the COVID-19 outbreak?

No. Check with the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, the California Franchise Tax Board, and your local government for updates.

Small business sales and use tax deferral

How can my business take advantage of sales and use tax relief from the California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)?

Contact CDTFA by visiting their COVID website or calling 800-400-7115.

When will small business sales and use tax relief payment plans begin?

The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration will begin accepting applications for interest-free payment plans in April 2021 for sales and use tax returns originally due between December 15, 2020 and April 30, 2021.

How will small business sale and use tax relief payment plans work?

Qualifying sales and use taxpayers with deferred liabilities of up to $50,000 will pay their tax due in equal monthly installments. No interest or penalties will be assessed against qualifying liabilities while they are in a tax-deferred payment plan.

What if small businesses owe more than the $50,000 limit on deferred sales and use tax?

The maximum amount that any taxpayer can defer interest-free under this relief effort is $50,000.

What if small businesses with more than $5 million in annual taxable sales need sales and use tax relief?

We recognize that some taxpayers, particularly in low margin businesses, with annual taxable sales over $5 million may also need this relief. The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration will work with these taxpayers to provide relief where appropriate.

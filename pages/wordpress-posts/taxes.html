California is providing tax relief during the pandemic.

Individuals

The California Earned Income Tax Credit and Young Child Tax Credit are now available to taxpayers who do not have an Individual Taxpayer Identifical Number (ITIN). These tax credits can be claimed on 2020 individual tax returns (which are filed in 2021).

People filing with an ITIN also no longer need to have a child under the age of six to claim the California Earned Income Tax Credit.

Small business taxes

California and the federal government are providing tax assistance and benefits to small businesses. This includes:

  • Interest-free deferral of sales/use tax up to $50,000 for businesses with less than $5 million in taxable sales
  • A $1,000 tax credit for new employees hired by small businesses (up to $100,000 per business)
  • Excluding forgiven Paycheck Protection Program loans from state gross income
  • Federal tax credits to small businesses for COVID-19 employee paid leave

Questions and answers

Are there any tax deductions for people affected by the COVID-19 outbreak?

No. Check with the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, the California Franchise Tax Board, and your local government for updates.

Small business sales/use tax deferral

How can my business take advantage of sales/use tax relief from the California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)?

Contact CDTFA by visiting their COVID website or calling 800-400-7115.

When will small business sales/use tax relief payment plans begin?

The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration is now accepting payment plan applications. Participating taxpayers must complete their payment plan by July 31, 2021.

How will small business sale/use tax relief payment plans work?

Qualifying sales and use taxpayers with deferred liabilities of up to $50,000 will pay their tax due in equal monthly installments. No interest or penalties will be assessed against qualifying liabilities while they are in a tax-deferred payment plan.

Are the small business sales/use tax deferral plans only for tax payments due for the first quarter of 2020?

The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration will work to accommodate qualified taxpayers who choose to use this program to distribute the burden of their tax return payments originally due between March and July 2020.

What if small businesses owe more than the $50,000 limit on deferred sales/use tax?

The maximum amount that any taxpayer can defer interest-free under this relief effort is $50,000.

What if small businesses with more than $5 million in annual taxable sales need sales/use tax relief?

We recognize that some taxpayers, particularly in low margin businesses, with annual taxable sales over $5 million may also need this relief. The California Department of Tax and Fee Administration will work with these taxpayers to provide relief where appropriate.

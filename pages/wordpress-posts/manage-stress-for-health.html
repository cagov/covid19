COVID-19 can impact our mental health as well as our physical health. If you are experiencing stress or anxiety as a result of the pandemic, you are not alone. Many Californians are coping with stress related to:

  • Economic distress due to lost wages
  • Employment
  • School closures
  • Social isolation due to physical distancing

Help for managing your stress

  • California Surgeon General’s Playbook: Stress Relief during COVID-19 (PDF)
    This guide provides actions you can take every day to support your mental and physical health.
  • UCLA Mindful (app)
    Practice mindfulness with this free app. This can lessen feelings of stress, and support your mental and physical health.
  • Mindshift (app)
    Take charge of anxiety with resources on this free app.

Help for parents and kids

Parents and families are facing a lot during this pandemic. Working moms and dads must now be workers, parents, child counselors, and teachers all at once. Some parents also face new work environments, job loss, and interpersonal violence. 

There are tools that can help parents and kids. Mental health professionals, researchers, and the California Surgeon General recommend the following resources.

Tools for families facing stress

  • Navigating the New Normal: Parents Edition (video)
    The California Surgeon General and experts talk about how to help little kids handle the stress of the pandemic. 
  • How to Support Our Kids During COVID-19 (Apple podcast)
    The California Surgeon General shares advice on
    • Talking to your kids about COVID-19
    • What we can do to help
    • How to mitigate stress at home
  • FOCUS on the GO! (app)
    This free game helps your family enhance and develop coping skills.
  • Young Children at Home During the COVID-19 Outbreak: The Importance of Self-Care (article)
    Taking care of yourself is an important part of parenting. This article contains ways to stay centered during COVID.

Tools for parents to help children

  • California Surgeon General’s Playbook: Stress Relief for Caregivers and Kids During COVID-19 (PDF)
    This guide identifies signs of stress and how to protect your family’s health. It is also available in:
    • Arabic
    • Chinese (Simplified and Traditional)
    • Korean
    • Spanish
    • Tagalog
    • Vietnamese
  • Trauma Informed Parent: Helping Adults and Children With Trauma During the Pandemic (video)
    The California Surgeon General provides tools for helping kids with trauma and stress. 
  • Emotional Wellbeing during the COVID-19 Pandemic (guide)
    Use these recommendations to help kids adjust to the pandemic.

Tools for families with school-related stress

  • School’s Out: A Parent’s Guide for Meeting the Challenge During the COVID-19 Pandemic (article)
    This article offers strategies to help kids learn at home. They include:
    • Establishing structure
    • Getting exercise and social contact
    • Setting aside appropriate leisure time
    • How to talk about COVID-19 with your kids
  • The Secret to Keeping Your Kids Happy, Busy and Learning if Their School Closes Due to Coronavirus (article)
    Routines help kids navigate the day. Learn how to establish a healthy routine for your family.
  • How LGBTQ Youth Can Cope with Anxiety and Stress During COVID-19 (blog post) 
    LGBTQ youth can face additional isolation and stress during COVID-19. The Trevor Project offers ways to manage emotions during the pandemic.

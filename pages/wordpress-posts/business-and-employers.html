California has resources to support workers and businesses financially impacted by COVID-19.

On this page:

  • Benefits for workers
  • Workplace safety
  • Financial assistance for businesses

Benefits for workers

There are several benefits available to workers impacted by COVID-19. You may be able to take advantage of: 

  • Unemployment insurance
  • Pandemic unemployment assistance
  • Paid sick and family leave
  • Disability insurance
  • Workers’ compensation

Information about these benefits is available in this chart from the Labor and Workforce Development Agency. 

Unemployment due to COVID-19

Several unemployment benefit programs and extensions are available if you lost your job or had your hours reduced due to COVID-19. Visit EDD’s website to learn about COVID-19 and unemployment benefits, and to find out how you can apply. 

Sick leave and other benefits

If you’re employed and have COVID-19, stay home. Options are available if you cannot work because you (or a family member you’re taking care of) are sick or quarantined. You may also be able to use paid sick leave to go to your vaccine appointment or if you experience vaccine-related symptoms. 

Learn about leave benefits in the:

  • Worker leave and pay benefits related to COVID-19 
  • Paid leave benefits chart from the Department of Industrial Relations 

For more information, review the Workers page of saferatwork.ca.gov.

Read about keeping workplaces safe during the COVID-19 pandemic while upholding civil rights in the Department of Fair Employment and Housing’s FAQs.

More help

If you’re looking for help with:

  • Paying your rent, eviction protection, or mortgage relief, visit the Housing page
  • Getting or affording food, visit the Food page
  • Finding a childcare provider, visit mychildcare.ca.gov
  • Finding more financial assistance, visit the Financial help page

Workplace safety

Businesses can keep their employees and customers safe from COVID-19 by using the guidance and resources available at saferatwork.ca.gov. Find information about workplace safety requirements, paid sick leave, and more.

Financial assistance for businesses

Business owners who have been financially impacted by COVID-19 can:

  • Prevent layoffs through work sharing programs 
  • Take advantage of extended filing deadlines for taxes and fees owed to the Department of Tax and Fee Administration
  • Claim federal tax credits like the Employee Retention Credit (available through June 30, 2021) and paid leave credit for vaccines (available through September 30, 2021)

Check business.ca.gov  for other programs that may be available for businesses.

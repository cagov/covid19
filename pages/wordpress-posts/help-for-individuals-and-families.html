If you’ve been financially affected by COVID-19, you may be eligible for:

  • Unemployment insurance
  • Paid sick leave
  • Golden State Stimulus payments
  • Food
  • Housing, rent, and utilities
  • Healthcare and health insurance
  • Child-specific benefits
  • Funeral costs

Unemployment insurance

If you lost your job, had your hours reduced, or your business was affected by COVID-19, you may be eligible for unemployment insurance benefits.

Some federal unemployment benefits ended September 2021. This includes:

  • Pandemic unemployment assistance (ended September 4, 2021)
  • Pandemic additional compensation (ended September 4, 2021)
  • Pandemic emergency unemployment compensation (ended September 4, 2021)
  • Mixed earner unemployment compensation (ended September 4, 2021)
  • Federal-state extended duration (ended September 11, 2021)

Paid sick leave and other benefits 

If you cannot work because you (or a family member you’re taking care of) are sick or quarantined due to COVID-19, there are options:

  • Review your paid sick leave options
  • Check if you may be eligible for paid family leave
  • Worker leave and pay benefits related to COVID-19
  • Compare paid leave options
  • Use the navigator tool to check if you may be eligible for California COVID-19 supplemental paid sick leave

You may be able to use paid sick leave or COVID-19 supplemental paid sick leave to:

  • Go to your vaccine appointment
  • If you experience vaccine-related side effects

If you cannot work due to, or are recovering from, COVID-19 illness, you may be eligible for: 

  • Disability insurance
  • Workers’ compensation

Find information about all of these benefits in this chart. 

Check the FAQs about these topics:

  • Job-protected unpaid leave (page 5)
  • Reasonable accommodation for a disability due to COVID-19 (pages 5-7)

Hotel rooms for healthcare workers

On June 15, 2021, the Hotel rooms for California healthcare workers program was discontinued for all facilities except those located in Riverside, Imperial, and San Diego counties. 

Help for agricultural workers

California offers in-home quarantine support in participating counties for workers who need to self-isolate for up to 14 days. Hotel rooms are also offered in some areas. See Help for agricultural workers.

Golden State Stimulus payments

Households earning $75,000 or less may qualify for at least one type of Golden State Stimulus (GSS) payment. This includes undocumented workers. Families with children may receive an increased payment. 

There are two types of GSS payments, GSS I and GSS II.

Deadline to qualify

You must file your 2020 tax return by October 15, 2021. 

  • Your 2020 tax return determines if you’re eligible to receive GSS payments. 
  • If you qualify, you’ll receive your payments by direct deposit or a check in the mail. 
  • If you’ve already filed your 2020 tax return, you do not have to do anything else.

Healthcare and health insurance

Community health clinics

Community health centers and clinics offer free or low-cost medical services. Find a health center in your area.

Health insurance

You may be eligible for free or low-cost health insurance through Medi-Cal. Apply at BenefitsCal.

If you received unemployment insurance, you may be eligible for health insurance for as low as $1 per month through Covered California. 

Child-specific benefits

CalWORKs cash aid

Families with children may be eligible for cash aid and services through the CalWORKs program. Families that qualify receive money each month to help pay for housing, food, and other necessary expenses. 

Families that have little or no cash can receive up to $200 immediately if they have a family emergency like:

  • No food
  • No essential clothing
  • Urgent medical needs
  • No place to stay

Apply at BenefitsCal or contact the social services agency in your county. 

Child tax credit

You can receive the Child Tax Credit (CTC) if you have children, even if you make little or no money. 

Monthly payments

You may be eligible to receive monthly payments for each child from July 2021 through December 2021:

  • Up to $300 per child under age 6
  • Up to $250 per child ages 6 to 17

Who qualifies

You may qualify if you have children under age 18 that live with you most of the time, and you earn up to:

  • $150,000 per year (joint filers)
  • $112,500 per year (single head of household)
  • $75,000 per year (other single filers)

Find out more about who qualifies for CTC advance payments. 

How to get the CTC

You do not have to do anything to receive the CTC payments if you have done one of these:

  • Filed a 2019 or a 2020 tax return
  • Gave the IRS your information using a non-filer portal
  • Gave the IRS your information to receive the Economic Impact Payment

Check if you are enrolled to receive CTC monthly payments in the CTC Update Portal.

If you have not yet submitted your information to the IRS, you can file a simplified tax return to get the CTC.

Childcare

California has programs to help families pay for childcare. Find out if you qualify for help paying for childcare or call 1-800-KIDS-793.

Funeral costs

Financial help is available for COVID-19 related funeral expenses.

To apply, call the COVID-19 funeral assistance helpline at 844-684-6333 (M-F, 6 am to 6 pm). You cannot apply online.

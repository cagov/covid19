What to do

Stay home if you’ve been exposed or have symptoms

Stay at home for 7 days if:

  • You have a high temperature (fever)
  • You have a new continuous cough
  • You have traveled recently to an area of concern [Link to map/CDC/WHO]
  • You have had contact with a person with COVID-19

It is not helpful to:

  • Wear a face mask if you are not sick or in a healthcare setting
  • Go to the doctor if you’re managing symptoms at home

Help prevent community spread

Older adults and people with serious medical conditions are most at risk of getting very sick from COVID-19. People can spread the disease even if they never develop symptoms, so the best way to protect your community is to stay healthy and be careful. Here’s how everyone can help:

  1. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water are not available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.
  2. Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth.
  3. Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces.
  4. Avoid contact with people who are sick, and stay home if you feel sick.
  5. Cough or sneeze into a tissue, then throw the tissue in the trash.
  6. Avoid large gatherings and follow guidance from local public health officials. For the latest, sign up for local emergency alerts.

Testing and treatment for COVID-19 symptoms

If you have symptoms, and

  • may have had contact with a person with COVID-19
  • or traveled recently to an area of concern

Contact your healthcare provider or your local health department

How is California responding?

On March 4, 2020, Governor Newsom declared a State of Emergency to help the state prepare for broader spread of COVID-19. On March 12, 2020, Governor Newsom issued an Executive Order in response to the continuing spread of COVID-19. This executive order:

  • Directs Californians to follow public health directives including canceling large gatherings of more than 250 people
  • Removes waiting period for unemployment and disability insurance for Californians who lose work as a result of the COVID-19 outbreak
  • Readies state to commandeer hotels & medical facilities to isolate & treat COVID-19 patients
  • Allows local and state legislative bodies to hold meetings via conference calls while still meeting state transparency requirements

All levels of state government are being deployed to tackle the evolving situation:

The California Department of Public Health (CDPH) is monitoring the disease across the state and publishing data regularly. See CDPH press releases for the latest news and data.

The California Business, Consumer Services and Housing Agency provides guidance for homeless providers and shelters (pdf).

Eighteen public health labs in California are testing for COVID-19, and soon even more will be. This means getting test results sooner, so patients get the best care.

The State is working to ensure all have access to healthcare:

Healthcare Options for Uninsured Individuals

If you do not have a source of health care coverage or have recently lost your employer-sponsored health care coverage, here are some options you can explore:

  1. Covered CA: Check out the Covered California website or call 1-855-295-2023 to find a healthcare program that works for you.
  2. Medi-Cal: See if you qualify for Hospital Presumptive Eligibility (HPE). The HPE Program provides qualified individuals with immediate access to temporary, no-cost Medi-Cal while applying for permanent Medi-Cal coverage or other health coverage.

Review healthcare coverage options and contact the specific programs for more information.

Cost Sharing Waivers for Individuals with Health Insurance

The Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) and the California Department of Insurance (DOI) are taking action to ensure that cost does not prevent access to medically necessary screening and testing for COVID-19. It has directed all full-service commercial and Medi-Cal plans to immediately reduce cost-sharing (including, but not limited to, co-pays, deductibles, or coinsurance) to zero for all medically necessary screening and testing for COVID-19.

Learn more from the DMHC COVID-19 Screening and Testing All Plan Letter and the DOI COVID-19 Screening and Testing Bulletin.

School and work

All schools, workplaces, and businesses should follow best practices to prevent spreading the disease (pdf) and encourage employees to stay home if sick.

What schools can do

Different measures will be appropriate for each school or district. For an overview of the scenarios and detailed guidance for each, see the CDPH Guidance for Schools and Guidance for Colleges and Universities.

Information for workers and employers

The Employment Development Department (EDD) provides a variety of support services to individuals affected by COVID-19 in California.

The EDD coronavirus webpage and the EDD Coronavirus FAQ provides information for both workers and employers.

If you’re unable to work due to having or being exposed to COVID-19 (certified by a medical professional), you can file a Disability Insurance (DI) claim.

Best practices for businesses and organizations

Keep spaces clean

  • Promote “tap and pay” electronic payment to limit handling of cash
  • Disinfect surfaces like doorknobs, tables, desks, and handrails regularly
  • Increase ventilation by opening windows or adjusting air conditioning

Avoid crowding

  • Use booking and scheduling whenever possible to stagger customer flow
  • Use online transactions where possible
  • Consider limiting attendance at larger gatherings

For transportation businesses, taxis, and ride shares

  • Keep windows open when possible
  • Increase ventilation
  • Regularly disinfect surfaces

For additional information, see guidance from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC): Keeping Workplaces, Homes, Schools or Commercial Establishments Safe
